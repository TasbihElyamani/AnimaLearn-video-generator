Convolutional Neural Networks in Detail

CNNs use three key operations: convolution (learnable filters slide across input), pooling (downsampling to reduce spatial dimensions), and fully connected layers (classification). Convolutional layers detect local features: edges, textures, patterns. Deeper layers detect higher-level features (faces, objects). Data augmentation (flipping, rotation, cropping) prevents overfitting on image data. Batch normalization after convolutions stabilizes training.
