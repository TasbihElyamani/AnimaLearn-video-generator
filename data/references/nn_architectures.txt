Neural Network Architectures

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), introduced by Yann LeCun in the 1990s, use convolutional layers with learnable filters to detect features in grid-like data (images). Key architectures: LeNet-5 (1998), AlexNet (2012, ImageNet breakthrough), VGGNet (2014), ResNet (2015, skip connections). Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) process sequential data with hidden states. LSTMs (Hochreiter & Schmidhuber, 1997) solve vanishing gradients with gated mechanisms. The Transformer (Vaswani et al., 2017, 'Attention Is All You Need') uses self-attention to process sequences in parallel. Transformers power GPT, BERT, T5, and LLaMA. Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs, Goodfellow 2014) pit a generator against a discriminator. Autoencoders learn compressed representations. Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) operate on graph-structured data.
