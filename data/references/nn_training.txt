Neural Network Training

Training involves forward pass (computing predictions), loss calculation (measuring error), and backward pass (backpropagation of gradients). Backpropagation uses the chain rule of calculus to compute gradients of the loss with respect to each weight. Common loss functions: cross-entropy for classification, MSE for regression. Optimizers update weights: SGD (basic), Adam (adaptive learning rates, most popular), RMSprop, AdaGrad. Learning rate schedules (cosine annealing, warmup) improve convergence. Regularization techniques prevent overfitting: dropout (randomly zeroing neurons), weight decay (L2), batch normalization, data augmentation. Training proceeds in epochs (full passes through data) with mini-batches. Early stopping halts training when validation loss stops improving. Transfer learning fine-tunes pre-trained models on new tasks.
