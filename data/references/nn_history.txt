History of Neural Networks

The perceptron was invented by Frank Rosenblatt in 1958 at Cornell. Minsky and Papert's 1969 book showed perceptrons couldn't solve XOR, triggering the first AI winter. Backpropagation was popularized by Rumelhart, Hinton, and Williams in 1986. The 1990s saw CNNs (LeCun) and LSTMs (Hochreiter). The 2006 deep learning revival was led by Geoffrey Hinton's deep belief networks. The 2012 AlexNet breakthrough on ImageNet proved deep CNNs could achieve superhuman image classification, winning by a large margin. Google's DeepMind created AlphaGo in 2016. The 2017 Transformer paper by Vaswani et al. revolutionized NLP and led to BERT (2018), GPT-2 (2019), GPT-3 (2020), ChatGPT (2022), and GPT-4 (2023). The field continues to evolve with multimodal models, mixture-of-experts, and scaling laws.
