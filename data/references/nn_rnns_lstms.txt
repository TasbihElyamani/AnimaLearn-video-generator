Recurrent Neural Networks and LSTMs

RNNs process sequences by maintaining a hidden state h_t = f(W_h·h_{t-1} + W_x·x_t + b). They suffer from vanishing/exploding gradients over long sequences. LSTMs (1997) solve this with gated mechanisms: forget gate (what to discard), input gate (what to store), output gate (what to output), and a cell state that carries information across many time steps. GRUs (Gated Recurrent Units) simplify LSTMs with just reset and update gates. Bidirectional RNNs process sequences in both directions. Seq2seq models (encoder-decoder RNNs) power machine translation. Attention mechanisms (Bahdanau, 2014) let the decoder focus on relevant encoder states. While Transformers have largely replaced RNNs for NLP, LSTMs remain useful for time series and streaming applications.
