Activation Functions in Neural Networks

Activation functions introduce non-linearity. Without them, any deep network would reduce to a linear transformation. Sigmoid: σ(x) = 1/(1+e^(-x)), outputs 0-1, suffers from vanishing gradients. Tanh: outputs -1 to 1, zero-centered but still has vanishing gradients. ReLU (Rectified Linear Unit): f(x) = max(0,x), computationally efficient, most popular, but 'dying ReLU' problem. Leaky ReLU: f(x) = max(αx, x) where α is small, prevents dying neurons. GELU (Gaussian Error Linear Unit): used in Transformers. Swish: f(x) = x·σ(x), smooth, used in EfficientNet. Softmax: converts logits to probabilities for multi-class classification. Choice of activation function significantly affects training speed and final performance.
