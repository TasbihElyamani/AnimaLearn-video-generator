Neural Networks: Overview

Artificial neural networks (ANNs) are computing systems inspired by biological neural networks in the brain. A neural network consists of layers of interconnected nodes (neurons): an input layer, one or more hidden layers, and an output layer. Each connection has a weight that is adjusted during training. The term was popularized by Frank Rosenblatt's perceptron in 1958. Modern deep learning uses networks with many hidden layers to learn hierarchical representations. Neural networks can approximate any continuous function (Universal Approximation Theorem). They are trained using backpropagation and gradient descent to minimize a loss function. Key hyperparameters include learning rate, batch size, number of layers, and neurons per layer. Neural networks require large datasets and significant computation (often GPUs or TPUs). They have achieved superhuman performance in image recognition, language understanding, and game playing.
